 10  /-!
 11  # The `simp_rw` tactic
 12  
 13  This module defines a tactic `simp_rw` which functions as a mix of `simp` and
 14  `rw`. Like `rw`, it applies each rewrite rule in the given order, but like
 15  `simp` it repeatedly applies these rules and also under binders like `∀ x, ...`,
 16  `∃ x, ...` and `λ x, ...`.
 17  
 18  ## Implementation notes
 19  
 20  The tactic works by taking each rewrite rule in turn and applying `simp only` to
 21  it. It should be possible to support backwards rewriting in the tactic, i.e.
 22  `simp_rw [←lemma]`, but it will be more useful and not much more work to add
 23  support for this directly to `simp`.
 24  -/
 25

 29  /-- `simp_rw` is a mix of `simp` and `rw`: it applies rewrite rules in the given order repeatedly,
 30    and can also rewrite under binders like `λ x, ...`
 31  
 32    Usage:
 33      - `simp_rw [l₁, ..., lₙ]` will rewrite the goal by applying lemmas `l₁, ..., lₙ` in that order.
 34      - `simp_rw [l₁, ..., lₙ] at h₁ ... hₙ` will rewrite hypotheses `h₁, ..., hₙ` in that order.
 35        Include `⊢` to also rewrite the goal.
 36      - `simp_rw [l₁, ..., lₙ] at *` will rewrite in the whole context.
 37  
 38    Lemmas passed to `simp_rw` must be expressions that are valid arguments to `simp`.
 39  -/
