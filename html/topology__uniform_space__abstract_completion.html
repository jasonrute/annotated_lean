 11  # Abstract theory of Hausdorff completions of uniform spaces
 12  
 13  This file characterizes Hausdorff completions of a uniform space α as complete Hausdorff spaces
 14  equipped with a map from α which has dense image and induce the original uniform structure on α.
 15  Assuming these properties we "extend" uniformly continuous maps from α to complete Hausdorff spaces
 16  to the completions of α. This is the universal property expected from a completion.
 17  It is then used to extend uniformly continuous maps from α to α' to maps between
 18  completions of α and α'.
 19  
 20  This file does not construct any such completion, it only study consequences of their existence.
 21  The first advantage is that formal properties are clearly highlighted without interference from
 22  construction details. The second advantage is that this framework can then be used to compare
 23  different completion constructions. See `topology/uniform_space/compare_reals` for an example.
 24  Of course the comparison comes from the universal property as usual.
 25  
 26  A general explicit construction of completions is done in `uniform_space/completion`, leading
 27  to a functor from uniform spaces to complete Hausdorff uniform spaces that is left adjoint to the
 28  inclusion, see `uniform_space/UniformSpace` for the category packaging.
 29  
 30  ## Implementation notes
 31  
 32  A tiny technical advantage of using a characteristic predicate such as the properties listed in
 33  `abstract_completion` instead of stating the universal property is that the universal property
 34  derived from the predicate is more universe polymorphic.
 35  
 36  ## References
 37  
 38  We don't know any traditional text discussing this. Real world mathematics simply silently
 39  identify the results of any two constructions that lead to something one could reasonnably
 40  call a completion.
