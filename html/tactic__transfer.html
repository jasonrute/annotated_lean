 13  /- Transfer rules are of the shape:
 14  
 15    rel_t : {u} Πx, R t₁ t₂
 16  
 17  where `u` is a list of universe parameters, `x` is a list of dependent variables, and `R` is a
 18  relation.  Then this rule will translate `t₁` (depending on `u` and `x`) into `t₂`.  `u` and `x`
 19  will be called parameters. When `R` is a relation on functions lifted from `S` and `R` the variables
 20  bound by `S` are called arguments. `R` is generally constructed using `⇒` (i.e. `relator.lift_fun`).
 21  
 22  As example:
 23  
 24    rel_eq : (R ⇒ R ⇒ iff) eq t
 25  
 26  transfer will match this rule when it sees:
 27  
 28    (@eq α a b)      and transfer it to    (t a b)
 29  
 30  Here `α` is a parameter and `a` and `b` are arguments.
 31
