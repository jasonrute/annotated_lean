 71  /-- Splits all if-then-else-expressions into multiple goals.
 72  
 73  Given a goal of the form `g (if p then x else y)`, `split_ifs` will produce
 74  two goals: `p ⊢ g x` and `¬p ⊢ g y`.
 75  
 76  If there are multiple ite-expressions, then `split_ifs` will split them all,
 77  starting with a top-most one whose condition does not contain another
 78  ite-expression.
 79  
 80  `split_ifs at *` splits all ite-expressions in all hypotheses as well as the goal.
 81  
 82  `split_ifs with h₁ h₂ h₃` overrides the default names for the hypotheses.
 83  -/
