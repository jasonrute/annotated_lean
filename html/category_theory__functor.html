  6  Defines a functor between categories.
  7  
  8  (As it is a 'bundled' object rather than the `is_functorial` typeclass parametrised
  9  by the underlying function on objects, the name is capitalised.)
 10  
 11  Introduces notations
 12    `C ⥤ D` for the type of all functors from `C` to `D`.
 13      (I would like a better arrow here, unfortunately ⇒ (`\functor`) is taken by core.)
 14  -/

 22  /--
 23  `functor C D` represents a functor between categories `C` and `D`.
 24  
 25  To apply a functor `F` to an object use `F.obj X`, and to a morphism use `F.map f`.
 26  
 27  The axiom `map_id_lemma` expresses preservation of identities, and
 28  `map_comp_lemma` expresses functoriality.
 29  -/
