  8  # Basic Definitions/Theorems for Continued Fractions
  9  
 10  ## Summary
 11  
 12  We define generalised, simple, and regular continued fractions and functions to evaluate their
 13  convergents. We follow the naming conventions from Wikipedia and [wall2018analytic], Chapter 1.
 14  
 15  ## Main definitions
 16  
 17  1. Generalised continued fractions (gcfs)
 18  2. Simple continued fractions (scfs)
 19  3. (Regular) continued fractions ((r)cfs)
 20  4. Computation of convergents using the recurrence relation in `convergents`.
 21  5. Computation of convergents by directly evaluating the fraction described by the gcf in
 22  `convergents'`.
 23  
 24  ## Implementation notes
 25  
 26  1. The most commonly used kind of continued fractions in the literature are regular continued
 27  fractions. We hence just call them `continued_fractions` in the library.
 28  2. We use sequences from `data.seq` to encode potentially infinite sequences.
 29  
 30  ## References
 31  
 32  - <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generalized_continued_fraction>
 33  - [Wall, H.S., *Analytic Theory of Continued Fractions*][wall2018analytic]

 71  /--
 72  A *generalised continued fraction* (gcf) is a potentially infinite expression of the form
 73  
 74                                  a₀
 75                  h + --------------------------
 76                                    a₁
 77                        b₀ + --------------------
 78                                      a₂
 79                              b₁ + --------------
 80                                          a₃
 81                                    b₂ + --------
 82                                        b₃ + ...
 83  
 84  where `h` is called the *head term* or *integer part*, the `aᵢ` are called the
 85  *partial numerators* and the `bᵢ` the *partial denominators* of the gcf.
 86  We store the sequence of partial numerators and denominators in a sequence of
 87  generalized_continued_fraction.pairs `s`.
 88  For convenience, one often writes `[h; (a₀, b₀), (a₁, b₁), (a₂, b₂),...]`.
 89  -/
