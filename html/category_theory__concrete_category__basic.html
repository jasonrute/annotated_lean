  9  # Concrete categories
 10  
 11  A concrete category is a category `C` with a fixed faithful functor
 12  `forget : C ⥤ Type*`.  We define concrete categories using `class
 13  concrete_category`.  In particular, we impose no restrictions on the
 14  carrier type `C`, so `Type` is a concrete category with the identity
 15  forgetful functor.
 16  
 17  Each concrete category `C` comes with a canonical faithful functor
 18  `forget C : C ⥤ Type*`.  We say that a concrete category `C` admits a
 19  *forgetful functor* to a concrete category `D`, if it has a functor
 20  `forget₂ C D : C ⥤ D` such that `(forget₂ C D) ⋙ (forget D) = forget
 21  C`, see `class has_forget₂`.  Due to `faithful.div_comp`, it suffices
 22  to verify that `forget₂.obj` and `forget₂.map` agree with the equality
 23  above; then `forget₂` will satisfy the functor laws automatically, see
 24  `has_forget₂.mk'`.
 25  
 26  Two classes helping construct concrete categories in the two most
 27  common cases are provided in the files `bundled_hom` and
 28  `unbundled_hom`, see their documentation for details.
 29
