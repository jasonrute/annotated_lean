 12  section induced

 14  /- Induced categories.
 15  
 16    Given a category D and a function F : C → D from a type C to the
 17    objects of D, there is an essentially unique way to give C a
 18    category structure such that F becomes a fully faithful functor,
 19    namely by taking Hom_C(X, Y) = Hom_D(FX, FY). We call this the
 20    category induced from D along F.
 21  
 22    As a special case, if C is a subtype of D, this produces the full
 23    subcategory of D on the objects belonging to C. In general the
 24    induced category is equivalent to the full subcategory of D on the
 25    image of F.
 26  
 27  -/
 28  
 29  /-
 30  It looks odd to make D an explicit argument of `induced_category`,
 31  when it is determined by the argument F anyways. The reason to make D
 32  explicit is in order to control its syntactic form, so that instances
 33  like `induced_category.has_forget₂` (elsewhere) refer to the correct
 34  form of D. This is used to set up several algebraic categories like
 35  
 36    def CommMon : Type (u+1) := induced_category Mon (bundled.map @comm_monoid.to_monoid)
 37    -- not `induced_category (bundled monoid) (bundled.map @comm_monoid.to_monoid)`,
 38    -- even though `Mon = bundled monoid`!
 39  -/
