 10  # Better `clear` tactics
 11  
 12  We define two variants of the standard `clear` tactic:
 13  
 14  * `clear'` works like `clear` but the hypotheses that should be cleared can be
 15    given in any order. In contrast, `clear` can fail if hypotheses that depend on
 16    each other are given in the wrong order, even if all of them could be cleared.
 17  
 18  * `clear_dependent` works like `clear'` but also clears any hypotheses that
 19    depend on the given hypotheses.
 20  
 21  ## Implementation notes
 22  
 23  The implementation (ab)uses the native `revert_lst`, which can figure out
 24  dependencies between hypotheses. This implementation strategy was suggested by
 25  Simon Hudon.
 26  -/
 27  
 28  open native tactic interactive lean.parser

 30  /-- Clears all the hypotheses in `hyps`. The tactic fails if any of the `hyps`
 31  is not a local or if the target depends on any of the `hyps`.
 32  
 33  If there are local hypotheses or definitions, say `H`, which are not in `hyps`
 34  but depend on one of the `hyps`, what we do depends on `clear_dependent`. If it
 35  is true, `H` is implicitly also cleared. If it is false, `clear'` fails. -/
